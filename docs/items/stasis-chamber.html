Stasis Chamber

Summary

The Stasis Chamber is a specialized medical containment unit that was built directly into the floor of the extraction helicopter used during Operation Living Bomb. It functions as a mobile medical containment and sedation system, capable of keeping prisoners or high-risk subjects unconscious during transport via gaseous sedative delivery.

The chamber was deployed to maintain The Exploding Man in an unconscious state throughout the evacuation. While the exact specifications and capabilities of the chamber remain unknown to the team, it clearly represents significant investment by Voss Dynamics in specialized containment and transport infrastructure, suggesting that high-risk extractions may be a recurring operational requirement.

Properties / Stats

Containment Capacity:

  • Single large humanoid subject
  • Sealed environmental system
  • Integrated into helicopter floor structure

Sedation System:

  • Gas-based unconsciousness induction and maintenance
  • Successfully maintained The Exploding Man in unconscious state during transport
  • Precise dosage control capabilities (implied)

Installation:

  • Built-in component of extraction helicopter
  • Suggests purpose-built equipment rather than field-adapted system
