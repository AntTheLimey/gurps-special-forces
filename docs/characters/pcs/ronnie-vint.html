Ronnie Vint grew up rough in a council estate somewhere best not mentioned. He joined the army young — it was that or prison, and the recruiting sergeant made a convincing case. Turns out the British Army had accidentally acquired one of the most naturally talented combat soldiers of his generation. He sailed through basic training, excelled in his regiment, and passed SAS selection on his first attempt.

The problem is everything else about him.

Ronnie steals. Not diamonds-and-vault-heists stealing — he’s petty. Cigarettes, ration packs, ammunition that can be sold, vehicle parts, booze from the officers’ mess. He runs card games, loan-sharks squaddies, sells duty-free tabs at markup, and knows a man who knows a man for anything you might need. Every base he’s posted to develops a thriving black market economy with Ronnie at its centre.

He bullies junior soldiers. He mouths off to officers with a smirk that’s technically within regulations. He gets into fistfights in pubs. He’s been busted from Corporal to Private (Trooper, technically, in the SAS) more times than anyone can remember, and he’ll probably be busted again.

But put him in a room that needs clearing, a convoy that needs ambushing, or a firefight that’s going sideways? Ronnie Vint is the man you want. Fast, fearless, lucky, and utterly unflappable. He’s been shot twice, stabbed once, blown up by an IED, and walked away from all of it with nothing worse than interesting scars and a bad attitude.

His CO finally found a solution: assign him to the defense contractor program and make him someone else’s problem. Ronnie doesn’t care. New posting means new opportunities.

Common tactical sequences Ronnie would use in play. Each “chain” lists the maneuvers, rolls, and order for a multi-turn combat action. References: GURPS Basic Set — Campaigns, Chapters 11-13; GURPS Martial Arts.

Chain 1: Dynamic Entry — Breach and Clear (2-3 Turn)

Situation: Ronnie kicks or blows a door, enters the room, and engages hostiles. Classic SAS CQB.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Attack Breach door (kick or shotgun) Forced Entry or Guns (Shotgun) 14 Shotgun hinges or kick the frame. Guy handles explosive breaching.
2 Move & Attack Enter room, engage first target Guns (Rifle) 16 - 4 (Move & Attack) = 12 First man through. -4 for M&A, or All-Out for full skill but no defense.
3 Attack Engage second target Guns (Rifle) 16 - range mod Stack clears the angles. Ronnie takes the “fatal funnel” because he’s best at it.

Alternative: Use All-Out Attack (Determined) for +1 to hit on entry, accepting no defense. With CR and HPT, Ronnie can often afford it.

Chain 2: Close Quarters — Rifle to Pistol Transition (2 Turn)

Situation: Rifle jams, runs dry, or target is too close for a long arm. Transition to pistol.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Ready Drop rifle (free), draw pistol Fast-Draw (Pistol) default DX-4 = 10 Rifle on sling drops. If Fast-Draw fails, costs another Ready.
2 Attack Fire pistol at target Guns (Pistol) 16 - range mod SIG P226, point blank. Effective 16 at contact range.

Upgrade path: Buy Fast-Draw (Pistol) to eliminate the fumble risk. At Ronnie’s DX 14, 1 point gets it to DX+0 = 14.

Chain 3: Silent Sentry Removal (2-3 Turn)

Situation: Ronnie approaches an unaware sentry from behind and eliminates them silently. SAS bread and butter.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Move Approach from behind Stealth 15 vs. target’s Perception Must win to avoid alerting target.
2 Attack Grab and apply Choke Hold Judo 14 to grapple, then Choke Hold 14 Target resists with ST or HT each turn. 5+ turns = unconsciousness, 10+ = death.
Alt Attack Knife to throat/neck Knife 16 at neck (-5) = 11 Cutting to throat: sw-1 cut x2 (neck multiplier). Often a one-hit kill.

Choke is silent but slow. Knife is fast but risks noise if they have time to scream. Ronnie’s call.

Chain 4: Arm Lock and Control (2-3 Turn)

Situation: Subdue and restrain a target without killing them. Prisoner capture.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Attack Judo grapple Judo 14 Establish hold. Target can try to break free.
2 Attack Apply Arm Lock Arm Lock 16 Target contests with ST, DX, or best grappling skill.
3 — Maintain and restrain Arm Lock 16 vs. target’s ST Cuff them while locked. Pain compliance if needed.

Arm Lock 16 is very high — Ronnie rarely loses the contest. Once locked, the target isn’t going anywhere.

Chain 5: Karate Combination — Aggressive Striking (2 Turn)

Situation: Close quarters, no weapon available or impractical. Beat them down with striking.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Attack Knee Strike (inside range) Knee Strike 15 thr cr + 1/die (Karate bonus) = 1d+1 cr. Target Reach C.
2 Attack Follow-up punch or elbow Karate 15 or Elbow Strike 14 1d cr punch (+1/die bonus). Press the advantage.

Alternative: Rapid Strike (two attacks, both at -6): Karate 15 - 6 = 9 for both punches. Only if you need to drop someone fast and can accept the hit penalty.

Chain 6: Knife Work — Close and Personal (1-2 Turn)

Situation: Ronnie’s favourite CQB tool after his rifle. Fast, silent, personal.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 Attack Knife thrust to torso Knife 16 thr imp = 1d-1 imp. Vitals (-3) = 13 for x3 multiplier.
Alt Attack Knife thrust to neck Knife 16 - 5 = 11 thr imp to neck, x2 wounding. Often fatal.
2 Attack Follow-up slash Knife 16 sw-1 cut = 1d cut. Target the arm to disable.

Knife 16 with Karate backup makes Ronnie lethal at contact range. He can punch, grapple, or stab depending on what the situation needs.

Chain 7: Vehicle Assault / Drive-by (Mobility Troop)

Situation: Ronnie driving, engaging from vehicle or through window.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 — Driving Driving (Auto) 15 Maintain control, position vehicle
2 Attack Fire from moving vehicle Guns (Pistol) 16 - 2 (moving vehicle) -2 for firing from moving platform. One-handed shooting.

Better option: Stop the vehicle, dismount, engage from stable position. Moving vehicle fire is for emergencies.

Chain 8: Ambush — Concealed Position with Rifle (Overwatch)

Situation: Ronnie has set up in cover and waits for targets to enter the kill zone.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
Prep — Move to position, ready weapon Stealth 15 Remain undetected.
1 Aim Aim at expected target location — +Acc (usually 4-5 for assault rifles). Can hold Aim.
2+ Wait “I fire when someone enters X” — Interrupt enemy turn when trigger met.
Trigger Attack Fire on target Guns (Rifle) 16 + Acc + additional Aim Likely 16 + 4 + 1 = 21 at short range. Devastating opener.

Ronnie at Guns 16 with aimed shot can hit almost anything at reasonable range. First shot from ambush is often the only shot needed.

Chain 9: Counter-Grapple — Someone Grabs Ronnie (Defensive)

Situation: Enemy gets hands on Ronnie. Use Judo to escape and reverse.

Turn Maneuver Action Roll Notes
1 — Enemy grapples Ronnie — Ronnie can parry at Judo 10 (Judo/2 + 3).
2 Attack Judo Throw (if grappled) Judo 14 Counter-throw them to the ground. They resist with ST, DX, or grappling.
Alt Attack Arm Lock reversal Arm Lock 16 Take control of the grapple. Contest to establish lock.
3 Attack Follow up with strike or choke Karate 15 (ground strike) or Choke Hold 14 Finish them on the ground.

High Pain Threshold means Ronnie ignores shock from any damage taken during the grapple. He can stay focused even if hurt.

Chain 10: Deliberate Clearing — Slow and Methodical (Extended)

Situation: Unknown structure, possible hostiles, no time pressure. SAS deliberate clearing.

Phase Action Skill Target Notes
1. Approach Move to entry point Stealth 15 Noise discipline.
2. Observe Check for threats through door/window Observation 11 Limited intel.
3. Prepare Stack, ready breach Tactics 11 Complementary: +1 to entry if success
4. Entry Breach and buttonhook Move & Attack Guns 12 Take the angles, pie the corners
5. Clear Systematic room search Observation 11 Check dead space, closets, under furniture
6. Report “Room clear” — — Move to next room

Ronnie’s Tactics 11 isn’t brilliant, but he knows the SAS drills cold. Muscle memory takes over.
